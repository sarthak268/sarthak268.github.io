*Conversations with scientists on life's biggest questions ⭐*

A collection of conversations with leading scientists and thinkers exploring the biggest questions about life, the universe, and what it means to be human.

Interviews

- Martin Rees — Cosmologist (on the beginning and end of the universe)
- Richard Dawkins — Evolutionary biologist (on egoism and selflessness)
- V. S. Ramachandran — Neuroscientist (on consciousness)
- Jared Diamond — Physiologist & geographer (on chance and necessity in history)
- Jane Goodall — Primatologist (on our relationship to animals)
- Steven Weinberg — Physicist (on science and religion)
- Elizabeth Blackburn — Molecular biologist (on aging)
- Peter Singer — Philosopher (on ethics)
- Nicholas Christakis — Physician & social scientist (on human relationships)
- Craig Venter — Biochemist (on the human genome)
- Roald Hoffmann — Chemist and poet (on beauty in molecules and science)
- Hannah Monyer — Neurobiologist (on memory)
- Vittorio Gallese — Neuroscientist (on empathy)
- Raghavendra Gadagkar — Animal behaviour researcher (on altruism)
- Ernst Fehr — Economist (on fairness)
- Walter Zieglgänsberger — Neuropharmacologist (on pain)
- Sarah Hrdy — Anthropologist (on motherhood)
- Alison Gopnik — Developmental psychologist (on childhood intelligence)
- Leonardo da Vinci — (on the beginnings of modern scientific research)
